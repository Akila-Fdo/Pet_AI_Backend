# Accumulation of Fluid or Air in the Chest Cavity in Dogs
Reviewed/Revised Jun 2018 | Modified Sept 2024
v3206524
**Hemothorax** , the accumulation of blood in the pleural (chest) cavity, is usually caused , blood clotting disorders, or tumors in the chest. **Hydrothorax** , the accumulation of clear fluid in the pleural cavity, is usually due to interference with blood flow or lymph drainage. **Chylothorax** , the accumulation of a high-triglyceride lymphatic fluid in the pleural cavity, is relatively rare in dogs. It may be caused 3 conditions include respiratory difficulty, such as rapid shallow breathing with labored inhalation, and weakness. Drainage of the fluid may be necessary to relieve these signs and can be helpful in diagnosing the underlying problem. However, the outlook for many disorders that cause accumulation of fluid in the chest is guarded to poor.
Air in the pleural cavity, called **pneumothorax** , may be caused , causing difficulty inhaling or rapid, shallow breathing. This condition should be considered an emergency that requires immediate veterinary attention.

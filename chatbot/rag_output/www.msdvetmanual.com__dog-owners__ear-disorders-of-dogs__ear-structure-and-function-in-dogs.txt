A dog's ear is responsible for hearing and balance. It has three parts: the outer ear, the middle ear, and the inner ear.
The **outer ear** includes the **pinna (the flap of cartilage covered ) and the ear canal**.
* The size and shape of the **pinna** vary * The dog’s **ear canal** is much longer than a human’s, which makes dogs better at detecting sound and able to hear higher frequencies. On average, dogs hear roughly 4 times better than people.

The **middle ear****contains the eardrum** and a small air-filled chamber with three tiny bones. It also contains two small muscles, an opening called the oval window, and the auditory tube, which links the middle ear to the back of the nose and lets air enter the middle ear.
The **inner ear** is complex and **includes the****cochlea****(the hearing center) and the system that controls balance**.

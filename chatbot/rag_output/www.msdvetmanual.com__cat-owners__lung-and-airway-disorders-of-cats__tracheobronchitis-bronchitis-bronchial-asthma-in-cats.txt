# Tracheobronchitis (Bronchitis, Bronchial Asthma) in Cats
Reviewed/Revised Aug 2018 | Modified Sept 2024
v3246569
Tracheobronchitis is an acute or chronic inflammation of the trachea and bronchial airways; it may also extend into the lungs. It is more likely to occur in cats already affected , parasites, , or ; it is most severe after rest or at the beginning of exercise. A slight fever may also be noted. The acute stage of bronchitis passes in 2 to 3 days, although the cough may persist for several weeks.
**Bronchial asthma** (allergic bronchitis) is a syndrome in cats with similarities to asthma in humans. Young cats and Siamese and Himalayan breeds are most often affected. The sudden development of asthma in older cats is extremely uncommon. The signs of bronchial asthma include shortness of breath, coughing, or wheezing that may come and go. However, a chronic cough in older cats is often due to pneumonia. If shortness of breath is severe, bluish mucous membranes (signaling a lack of oxygen in the blood) may be seen.
The veterinarian’s diagnosis is made from the history, physical examination, and clinical signs and -rays, use of an endoscope to view the bronchial tubes (bronchoscopy), and collection of biopsy and swab samples for laboratory analysis. These diagnostic tests may be needed in cases where the veterinarian’s initial treatment is not effective in providing relief.
In mild cases or those with a recent onset of signs, supportive therapy may be effective, but treatment of the underlying disease (if present) is also needed. Rest, warmth, and proper hygiene are important. If bacterial infection is present, broad-spectrum antibiotics may be prescribed. The veterinarian may perform or prescribe therapy such as use of a mist (nebulization) or steam from a hot shower to help loosen secretions, making them easier to cough up.
Feline asthma is treated with oral or inhaled corticosteroids. Aerosol chambers and specialized inhalers are available to administer inhaled medications in cats. Additional medications to open the airways or reduce inflammation may also be necessary. It is also important to avoid aerosolized products or allergens (for example, cigarette smoke, perfumes, pollens, molds, or dust) that may worsen the disease.
